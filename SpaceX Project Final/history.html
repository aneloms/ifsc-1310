SpaceX History

SpaceX, founded in 2002, began developing the Dragon spacecraft in late 2004 and made its public announcement in 2006, aiming to enter service by 2009. That same year, SpaceX won a contract with NASA for the Dragon spacecraft to provide commercial resupply services to the International Space Station (ISS).

In December 2010, the Dragon spacecraft made history as the first commercially-built and operated spacecraft to be recovered successfully from orbit. On May 25, 2012, Dragon became the first commercial spacecraft to rendezvous with and attach to the ISS. These milestones were part of SpaceX's ongoing role in NASA's Commercial Resupply Services program, with regular cargo flights to the ISS beginning in October 2012.

SpaceX's focus on reusability and cost reduction continued to drive innovation in the space industry. On June 3, 2017, SpaceX launched the CRS-11 mission, reusing components from the CRS-4 mission, including the hull, structural elements, and avionics. This mission further demonstrated SpaceX's commitment to sustainability and technological advancement in space exploration.
